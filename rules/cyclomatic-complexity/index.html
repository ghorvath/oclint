Cyclomatic Complexity

Cyclomatic complexity is determined by the number of decision points in a method plus one for the method entry. The decision points are ‘if’, ‘for’, ‘for-each’, ‘while’, ‘do’, ‘catch’, ‘case’, ‘&&’, ‘||’ and ‘?:’. A complexity higher than 7 will be considered as a violation to this rule.
